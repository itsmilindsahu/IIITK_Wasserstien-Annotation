What this actually does

Three fixes on top of the Shang et al. (AAAI-25) video summarization diffusion model, replacing operations that treat annotator importance scores as free vectors in R^N with ones that respect the fact they live on the probability simplex:

  1. Fix 1 — replace the arithmetic mean of annotators with a Wasserstein (Sinkhorn) barycenter.
  2. Fix 2 — replace Gaussian forward diffusion noise with Dirichlet noise + simplex projection.
  3. Fix 3 — add a Wasserstein-distance penalty term to the training loss.

On this synthetic two-camp video (half the annotators peak around frame 5–10, half around frame 20–25), the arithmetic mean stays bimodal while the barycenter smooths toward a single interpolated mode — the expected qualitative behaviour from Figure 1 of the proposal. In this particular run the barycenter's average W2 distance to the annotators came out higher than the arithmetic mean's under the simplified CDF-based W2 proxy used here, which is the opposite of what the theory predicts under the true W2 metric. Flagged as an open question for Phase 2 — likely the 1D "sum of squared CDF differences" proxy isn't equivalent to the true W2 the Sinkhorn barycenter actually minimizes. Reporting it as-is rather than smoothing it over.
